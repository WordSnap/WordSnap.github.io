About

Word Snap will be a fun and refreshing puzzle game based on spelling. The game engine and environment used to develop Word Snap will be the Community edition of the Unity engine. This will build on the team’s familiarity with the development environment

as this was used for each of the team’s individual games. The targeted platform will be android mobile for touch screen interaction. The final goal of the project will be the get Word Snap on the Google play store for monetization.

Word Snap!

The main design of Word Snap came from Matthew Landry’s love of puzzle mobile games, in particular Toy Blast. This game’s simple yet addictive design lead Matthew to spend countless hours in this mobile game. Landry, fueled by his love for this game, urged the group to set its sights towards a puzzle mobile game. With that rough outline for a type of game, the team begun white boarding game concepts.

Our Story

The core game will consists of three states. Title screen, main gameplay screen, and the game over/win screen. The title screen will contain the game name and logo as well as the company name, and a button to start the main gameplay screen. Within the main gameplay screen will be a grid of blocks with letters on them. Below the grid will be the player’s deck (ala scrabble) of letters the player can swap out with ones in the grid. If the player is able to form a word from our dictionary then the blocks will disappear and the player will be given points. Once there are no words left to make the game will throw to the game over screen and display the user’s final score.
